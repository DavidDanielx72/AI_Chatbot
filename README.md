## 📖 About the Project

Ubuntu Navigator is an AI-powered conversational assistant designed to help users search for answers and access information related to SASSA, Home Affairs, and services connected to the City of Cape Town.

It makes use of all 11 official languages of South Africa, so users can message in any of those languages and receive a response in the same language. It can answer a wide range of questions, provide helpful guidance, and support users with information based on their needs. Ubuntu Navigator can also help users find Home Affairs buildings near them by using their current location or a location they enter manually.

By making information easier to find and understand, Ubuntu Navigator helps users save time and get the support they need more quickly.

## 🎯 Problem Statement

Many South Africans face barriers when accessing government services due to:

* Long queues at government offices
* Expensive transport costs
* Complex legal and government terminology
* Limited access to reliable information
* Language barriers
* Uncertainty about required documentation

Our goal is to reduce these challenges by providing accessible, reliable, and easy-to-understand information through AI.

---

## 💡 Our Solution

Ubuntu Navigator serves as a digital assistant that helps users:

* ✅ Understand grant eligibility
* ✅ Prepare required documents
* ✅ Learn about South African ID applications
* ✅ Understand basic labour rights
* ✅ Receive information in plain language
* ✅ Avoid unnecessary trips to government offices

---

# 👥 Target Audience

Ubuntu Navigator is designed for South Africans aged **16 years and older**, including:

* Students applying for their first ID
* First-time SASSA applicants
* Job seekers
* Domestic workers
* Farm workers
* Parents applying for Child Support Grants
* Elderly citizens
* Individuals seeking basic legal guidance

# 🔒 AI Safety & Guardrails

Ubuntu Navigator has been designed to:

* Never fabricate legal information.
* Clearly communicate that it is **not an official government representative.**
* Encourage users to verify critical information with official government departments.
* Protect sensitive information such as ID numbers and personal details.
* Provide educational guidance rather than legal advice.
